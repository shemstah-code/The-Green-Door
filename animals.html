Who we care for

Dogs, cats, and mostly strays.

Every animal that comes through The Green Door gets food, vet care, and patience — for as long as it takes.

The animals

Who comes through our door

Most of the animals we care for are strays — dogs and cats found on the street, reported by community members, or rescued after a storm or disaster. A few come from owners who can no longer care for them.

We keep our intake small so every animal gets real attention, not just a cage and a bowl of food.

🐕
Dogs
Mostly strays or community dogs. Many arrive nervous, malnourished, or injured. We decompress them slowly before grooming, vet care, and eventually rehoming.
🐈
Cats
Street cats, abandoned kittens, and occasionally owner surrenders. Cats are housed separately and given quiet spaces to settle before socialisation begins.
How it works

From rescue to home

01
Rescue or intake
An animal is reported, found, or surrendered. We assess the situation and arrange safe pickup or transfer.
02
Vet check and stabilisation
Every animal gets a health check, vaccinations where needed, and treatment for injuries or illness before anything else.
03
Decompression and care
Quiet space, consistent food, gentle handling, and enrichment — including natural chews — help nervous animals settle and build trust.
04
Grooming and preparation
Once an animal is ready, grooming sessions help them look and feel their best before meeting potential adopters.
05
Adoption or foster placement
We match animals with the right home — whether that's a permanent adoption or a foster placement while we continue searching.
