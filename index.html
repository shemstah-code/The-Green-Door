Our mission

A small rescue with a soft spot for the island's strays.

From the team at The Green Door

The Green Door started the way most rescues do — one hungry dog at the gate, then another. Today we focus on a handful of animals at a time, so each one gets the food, vet care and patience they need before going to a new home.

Alongside the rescue work, we make small-batch natural chews and offer gentle grooming. Everything we sell helps fund the next rescue, the next vet visit, the next ride home.

The bone + snuggle effect

From side-eye to tail-wag in four tiny acts of care.

Rescue work is patient work: safety first, then food, comfort, grooming, and small happy moments. A good chew and a gentle cuddle can turn a worried tail into a wagging one.

Before
"Who are you?"
Many rescues need quiet, routine, and time before they can relax around people again.
Care
Bone + bath + brush + snuggle
Natural chews, gentle grooming, and kind hands help make care feel safe instead of scary.
After
"Okay, I live here now."
The goal: healthier pets, softer landings, and more happy animals ready for their next home.
01
Decompress first
A quiet corner, clean water, and predictable routine help a new rescue understand that nothing bad is coming next.
02
Offer a safe chew
Chewing gives dogs something natural to do with nervous energy, especially after a big change or grooming visit.
03
Go gentle with grooming
Short sessions, soft handling, and breaks turn brushing, bathing, and nail care into trust-building time.
04
End with affection
A snuggle, praise, or a calm sit-together helps the animal link care with comfort, not pressure.

Priority · Dogs + cats first

After Hurricane Mellissa, every dog and cat needs a safe next step.

Storms separate animals from homes, medicine, food, and the people who know them. The Green Door Jamaica is building a recovery push to rehome displaced pets, vaccinate at-risk animals, and spay or neuter dogs and cats so the crisis does not become a second wave of suffering.

Help the recovery push Report an animal in need
🏠
Rehome
Match displaced animals with foster homes, adopters, or safe temporary care.
💉
Vaccinate
Coordinate core vaccines and vet checks for dogs and cats exposed after the storm.
✚
Spay + neuter
Prevent accidental litters while families, shelters, and communities recover.
🥣
Feed + comfort
Provide food, chews, grooming, and calm handling so frightened animals can decompress.
